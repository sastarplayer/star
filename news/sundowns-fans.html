Sundowns fans called Pitso Mosimane by names.


By SA STARPLAYER Friday May-22, 21:30 PM
  Mdunge Skhumbuzo

Sundowns have (1-1) lock horns with Al Ahly in the Caf Champions League match that partook at the Lucas Moripe Stadium


In the first eleven minutes, Yasser Ibrahim opened the scoreline with a header flicked by Al Saluya from the corner kick. By 30 minutes later, Mosa Lebusa also retaliated with another goal, assisted by Aubrey Modiba from the corner kick. The draw has favoured Al Ahly to walk away with (3-1) aggregate score to the semi-finals.


Al Ahly coach, Pitso Mosimane, said he deserved to get respect from the Mamelodi Sundowns supporters.


"We're professionals, and this is a game of football, but I was a little bit emotional when I saw the placards outside. They wanted to stop the bus, all these people swearing at me and swearing at my mother. I said to myself, what else should I've done for this team to get the respect, but it's football, I understand."


Pitso Mosimane said the Sundowns fans were calling him by names. "It's football, I understand, and I know who did that. I know who is behind it. He's bitter, but I had to move on with my life. There is more, but I don't want to talk. The things that I receive before the match from the South African supporters, if I can show you my phone you can see, the corresponding and emails, they just don't want to let me go."


The Egyptian Red Devils are defending the title of Caf Champions League. According to Pitso, there lifted the trophy nine times and determined to make another star.
